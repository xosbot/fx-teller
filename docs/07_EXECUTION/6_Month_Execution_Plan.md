# FX Teller — 6-Month Execution Plan

**Version 1.0**
**Status:** Master execution document — the primary operating plan for the next six months
**Owner:** Founder, with the Strategy Office (CSO + Operations Excellence Consultant + Product Strategist) as custodian
**Audience:** Founder, current and future employees, Hosts, AI agents producing any future execution document

> This document is the definitive 6-month execution plan. It was produced by a 9-specialist subagent analysis (Business Strategy Consultant, Product Strategist, Operations Excellence Consultant, Brand Strategist, Financial Strategy Consultant, Behavioural Economist, Market Research Analyst, Legal & Compliance Advisor, Devil's Advocate) with the CSO synthesising the final unified plan. The document challenges the existing drafts where the subagents disagreed. The document is the week-by-week operating plan a premium lifestyle brand would actually use to launch.
>
> The plan treats the Founder as the most-load-bearing and most-fragile resource. The plan commits to 5 v1 services in 90 days. The plan commits to 25–35 Founding Members by Month 6 and 50 by Month 9. The plan commits to the legal stack (SEBI opinion, Member agreement stack, Host Voice Code, DPO) before Member #1. The plan commits to a 12-month Founder-capital reserve of ₹1.2–1.5 Cr. The plan commits to second Host and second writer by Month 4–5. The plan commits to the 3-rhythm Year-1 cadence (daily, weekly, fortnightly) and the 4th rhythm in Year 2.

## 1. Executive Summary

**Vision for the next six months.** FX Teller ships a 5-service v1 audio-first trading floor in 90 days, onboards 25–35 Founding Members by Month 6 and 50 by Month 9, and emerges at Month 6 with a 4-person team, a 3-rhythm operational cadence, a SEBI-defensible legal stack, a Member agreement stack, a data protection posture, a brand voice that is consistent across surfaces, and a Founding cohort that is, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single asset.

**Primary objective.** Ship the v1 product. Recruit the second Host and the second writer. Author the legal stack. Onboard the first 25 Founding Members. Establish the operational cadence. The objective is operational, not financial; the financial outcome follows the operational outcome.

**Critical success factors.** Five. (1) The Founder's writing engine runs at 2 essays/week for 6 months. (2) The second Host and the second writer are in the seat by Month 4–5. (3) The legal stack is Member-1-ready, not Month-6-ready. (4) The first Floor session produces a warm, not austere, Member experience. (5) The 4-person team runs a 3-rhythm cadence (daily, weekly, fortnightly) that is honoured, not aspirational.

**Major risks.** Five. (1) Founder burnout by Month 3 (the most-likely catastrophic failure). (2) v1 mobile product delayed past Month 3. (3) SEBI enforcement on Floor content. (4) Working Professional conversion below 1%. (5) Documentation discipline sacrificed under launch pressure.

**Definition of success.** At Month 6: 25–35 Founding Members (Expected case; 50 Optimistic case); 30-day retention at 75%+ (Conservative), 85% (Expected), 92% (Optimistic); 12-month run rate of the retention curve measurable against the Design Partner cohort; second Host and second writer in seat; legal stack signed by external counsel; first in-person Founder dinner completed; Year-2 strategic capital raise preparation initiated.

The plan is, in the long run, a Founder-protection plan disguised as a launch plan. The plan is, in the long run, the discipline of doing the operations well. The plan is, in the long run, the only test that matters.

## 2. Guiding Principles

The 6-month plan is governed by 8 guiding principles. The principles are operational, not aspirational; each is enforced in a specific month, in a specific decision, in a specific artefact.

**1. Execution over perfection.** A 70%-quality v1 in 90 days beats a 100%-quality v1 in 180 days. The plan is, in the long run, a launch plan, not a polishing plan. A Founder who is still refining the spec in Month 2 has, in effect, missed the launch.

**2. Member-first.** Every operational decision is run through the test: does this serve the long-term Member or the short-term metric? The Member is the only legitimate measure. A decision that improves a metric at the cost of a Member is rejected.

**3. Launch early.** The Design Partner Program launches in Month 2, the Founding cohort begins in Month 4, the public surface is, in the long run, a Year-2 event. The v1 launches with 8–15 design-fit people, not 1,000 strangers. The early launch is the brand's most-defensible single risk-reduction move.

**4. Iterate quickly.** The product releases weekly from Month 2 onward. The Host reviews every Floor. The Founder reviews every application. The Decision Log is updated weekly. A weekly iteration cadence is, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single operational discipline.

**5. Protect trust.** Trust is the brand's most-defensible single asset. A SEBI event, a data breach, a marketing misstep, a discount, a community failure is, in the long run, a trust destruction. The plan protects trust by sequencing the legal stack before the Founding cohort, by recording every material decision, by enforcing the "what we are NOT" list per-surface, by the Founder's personal response to the first 30 days of every Member.

**6. Measure everything.** The 7-day operational KPIs are tracked daily. The 7-day operational metrics are tracked weekly. The 6-dimension quality bar is reviewed monthly. The 20 operational principles are reviewed quarterly. A measurement that is not tracked is, in effect, a measurement that has not happened.

**7. Keep operations lean.** The 4-person team is the Year-1 ceiling. The plan does not commit to a 5th hire. The plan commits to fractional Operations at 2–3 days/week. The plan commits to the second Host by Month 4–5 only if the Headcount Rule funds the role; the Founder subsidises the gap in Months 2–4.

**8. Expand only after validation.** The 50-Member Founding cohort completes by Month 9, not Month 6. The public surface is a Year-2 event. The Dubai entity is a Year-3+ event. The Year-2 strategic capital raise is the discipline that funds the House, the AI Companion, and the international expansion. A Founder who is tempted to expand earlier is a Founder who is, in the long run, expanding the philosophy's surface faster than the philosophy can defend it.

## 3. Six-Month Objectives

The 6-month objectives are organised by 10 dimensions. Each dimension has a Month-6 target, a trajectory across the 6 months, and a primary bottleneck.

**Product.** Month 6: 5 daily-cadence services (Trading Floor, Morning Floor Briefing, New York Session, Closing Bell, Market Commentary) + 4 monthly/quarterly services (Weekly Review, Process Journal, Member Introductions, Founding rituals) + 4 aspirational services (Private Group Sessions, Trading Reviews, Expert Roundtables, Member Spotlights) = 13 services in the catalogue, 5 in v1, 9 by Month 6. Trajectory: 0 (M1) → 5 (M3) → 9 (M5) → 13 (M6). Bottleneck: the engineering team is one full-time contractor at Month 1.

**Members.** Month 6: 25–35 Founding Members (Expected case). Trajectory: 0 → 5 Design Partners (M3) → 15 (M4) → 25 (M5) → 35 (M6). Bottleneck: the application funnel (writing reach → application starts → approval) is Founder-dependent and the application-to-approval ratio has never been measured.

**Revenue.** Month 6: MRR = 35 Founding × Annual price ÷ 12 (at ₹50,000/year Annual, MRR ≈ ₹1.46L). Trajectory: ₹0 (M1–2) → first MRR at M3 (Design Partner cohort) → ₹X by M6. Bottleneck: the Annual price is OPEN; the 70% Annual-tier penetration assumption is unproven at any Indian price point.

**Marketing.** Month 6: 20+ Substack posts, 1,000 subscribers, 5,000+ LinkedIn cumulative connections, 5,000–20,000 awareness reach, application funnel instrumented. Bottleneck: the Founder's 8–10 writing hours/week is the entire growth engine; no second writer until Month 4–5.

**Operations.** Month 6: 6 SOPs documented, 4 checklists in daily use, incident-response protocol tested once, Decision Log operational, 3-rhythm cadence honoured (daily, weekly, fortnightly). Bottleneck: the Founder is the only operations person; fractional Operations at 2 days/week.

**Technology.** Month 6: v1 mobile on Android (private), v1.1 with CarPlay voice, session replay, Mentor pairing, 95%+ Floor uptime. Bottleneck: SEBI-safe narration tooling has never been built.

**Compliance.** Month 6: SEBI opinion in writing, Member agreement stack (6 documents) signed by counsel, DPDP-compliant privacy posture, DPO named, audit trails retained for 7 years. Bottleneck: external counsel timeline is exogenous; this is the existential dependency.

**Community.** Month 6: 25–35 Members in 2 cohorts of 12–17, with first in-person Founder dinner, weekly debrief, first Mentor pairings. Bottleneck: every new Member's first 30 days is Founder-moderated by Year-1 design.

**Brand.** Month 6: the "members' club for serious retail traders" category lodged in the Founder's writing voice and the first 35 Members' testimonials. Bottleneck: brand consistency under Founder-bandwidth pressure.

**Finance.** Month 6: 12-month cash runway (6 months forward of Month 6), 35 Members × Annual ÷ 12 MRR, Headcount-Rule safety factor documented, Year-2 strategic-capital conversation initiated. Bottleneck: Conservative case requires ₹50–65L 6-month deployment; the Founder is the backer.

## 5. Weekly Sprint Structure

The weekly sprint structure is the rhythm that compounds. The rhythm is, in the long run, the discipline that produces consistency, and the consistency is, in the long run, what produces trust.

**Monday — Planning (30 min, async).** Owner: Founder + fractional Operations. Output: written weekly plan with: top 3 priorities, decisions to be made, decisions deferred, decisions recorded. Failure: drift into a status call. Recovery: every Monday produces a 1-page written plan, in the same template.

**Tuesday — Execution (full day, async).** Owner: Founder (writing day, 4–6 hours of essays + 1–2 hours of editing). Engineer (code day). Host (Floor prep, if a session is scheduled). Output: 1 essay published, code shipped, Floor prep complete. Failure: drift into meetings. Recovery: Tuesday is, in the long run, the no-meeting day.

**Wednesday — Product Review (60 min, sync).** Owner: Founder + Engineer. Output: v1 build progress, Design Partner feedback, one Decision Log entry, one philosophy test result. Failure: drift into status reporting. Recovery: every Wednesday produces one written decision, not five.

**Thursday — Growth (full day, async).** Owner: Founder (writing, 2 essays/week cadence; second essay published this week). Host (Floor). Output: 1 essay published, second Floor session of the week, second Substack/LinkedIn post. Failure: drift into operational fires. Recovery: Thursday is the writing day for the second essay, no operational fires.

**Friday — Metrics (30 min, sync).** Owner: Founder. Output: 7-day operational KPIs, weekly Metrics Review, weekly Decision Log entry, weekly note. Failure: drift into status reporting. Recovery: every Friday produces a 1-page written note with the 7-day numbers, the 30-day trend, and one written decision.

**Saturday — Community (full day, async).** Owner: Founder (community moderation, Member spotlights, Mentor pairing). Host (Floor prep, async Member interactions). Output: weekly debrief hosted (Saturday morning), Member Introductions, Community rituals. Failure: drift into operational work. Recovery: Saturday is the community day, no operational fires.

**Sunday — Leadership Review (90 min, every two weeks).** Owner: Founder + fractional Operations. Output: fortnightly memo, Decision Log review, system-change decision, post-incident review. Failure: drift into strategic brainstorm. Recovery: fixed agenda — numbers, decisions, lessons, philosophy test. The 4th rhythm (monthly) is added in Year 2 with the 6-person team; the 4th rhythm (quarterly) is added in Year 3 with the 8-person team.

The weekly rhythm is, in the long run, the most-defensible single operational discipline the plan commits to. A rhythm that is not honoured is, in the long run, a rhythm that has taught the team to ignore rhythms.

## 6. Team Plan

**Founder.** The Founder is, in the long run, the load-bearing single resource for: writing (2 essays/week, ~10 hours/week), application review (5-day SLA, 5–10 hours/week), first-30-day moderation (5–7 hours/week, Years 1–1.5), Floor presence (1–2 sessions/week, 5–10 hours/week), strategic decisions (5–7 hours/week), incident response (3–5 hours/week), Ops leadership (5–7 hours/week), leadership review (fortnightly, 90 min), quarterly review (4×/year, 90 min). Total: 45–55 hours/week, hard ceiling. The Founder is, in the long run, the bottleneck, the most-fragile resource, and the most-load-bearing asset.

**Operations (fractional, 2–3 days/week).** The fractional Operations person owns: Member support (5–8 hours/week), weekly metrics (2–3 hours/week), monthly memo (4 hours/month), incident post-mortems (1–2 hours/incident), vendor management (1–2 hours/week). The fractional role expands to 3 days/week by Month 3 to support the first 30-day moderation at higher quality.

**Technology (full-time, Engineer).** The Engineer owns: v1 mobile build (Months 1–3), v1.1 features (Months 4–5), v1.5 features (Month 6), 100ms.live integration, Razorpay integration, OVH Singapore infrastructure, on-call rotation, SRE, support bug triage. The Engineer is, in the long run, the SPOF for v1 mobile; a 2-month buffer of cross-training is the only mitigation.

**Host (full-time).** The first Host owns: Floor narration (1 daily service at quality, expanding to 2 with the second Host), Floor preparation, post-Floor notes, Community rituals, off-Floor writing (1 essay/month, growing), Member moderation. The second Host is hired in Month 4–5 and shares Floor duties.

**Marketing (Founder-only in Year 1).** The Founder owns: writing (2 essays/week), Substack (1,000 subscribers), LinkedIn (5,000 connections), X (1 post/week), in-person events, brand surface. The second writer is hired in Month 4–5 to share the writing engine.

**Support (Mentor pool in Year 1).** The Mentor pool owns: first-30-day moderation, weekly debrief moderation, Member Introductions, accountability circles. The Mentor pool is, in the long run, 2–3 senior Members, recruited from the Founding cohort, with the Founder's personal moderation in Months 1–3 and the Host's moderation in Months 4+.

**Future hiring (Year 2).** Dedicated Operations (3 days/week → full-time, 150–200 Members). Second Engineer (Year 2, 250–300 Members). Head of Community (Year 2, 300+ Members). Head of Marketing (Year 3, 500+ Members). The hiring is, in the long run, Member-count-gated, not calendar-gated.

## 8. Marketing Execution

## Strategic Observations

**Observation 1 — The Founder is the most-load-bearing and most-fragile resource.** The 4-person team's Year-1 capacity is, in the long run, a 1.5-person full-time team. The Founder's 45–55 hours/week is the hard cap, not the target. A Founder who is, in effect, doing 4 jobs is a Founder who is, in effect, one burnout away from collapse. The plan protects the Founder by hiring the second Host and second writer in Month 4–5, by capping the Founding cohort at 25–35 by Month 6, by sequencing the legal stack before the Founding cohort, and by committing to a 3-rhythm cadence before the 4th.

**Observation 2 — The legal stack is the existential dependency.** A SEBI enforcement action in Year 2 is a 5–10× cost-of-capital event. The legal stack is Member-1-ready, not Month-6-ready. The Host Voice Code, the SEBI opinion, the Member agreement stack, the DPO, the data protection posture, the audit trails are, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single surface — and the surface is, in the long run, the surface most-likely to be compromised under launch pressure.

**Observation 3 — The 25–35 Founding Members by Month 6 is the realistic commitment.** The 50 by Month 6 is the Optimistic case; the 50 by Month 9 is the Expected case. The plan commits to 25–35 by Month 6 (Founding cohort first cohort) and 50 by Month 9 (Founding cohort complete). A Founder who promises 50 by Month 6 is a Founder who is, in the long run, over-promising; the 50-Member cap is, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single scarcity signal, and the scarcity signal cannot be produced under pressure.

**Observation 4 — The writing engine is the brand's only growth surface that scales without violating the philosophy.** 2 essays/week Founder + 1 essay/month Host + 1 essay/month second writer = 100+ essays in 6 months. The writing is, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single growth surface. A writing engine that fails is a brand that fails. A second writer in Month 4–5 is, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single growth-surface diversification.

**Observation 5 — The Founder's first-30-day moderation is the brand's most-defensible single trust surface.** The first 30 days is the identity-formation phase. The Founder's personal response to the first 25 Members is, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single trust action. The cutover at Member 25 — to Host-moderated, with a visible Founder artefact (a Founder essay, a Founder video, a Founder note) — is, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single transition. A cutover that is silent is, in the long run, a retreat the brand cannot defend.

**Observation 6 — The 3-rhythm cadence is the Year-1 operational reality.** The 4-rhythm cadence is the Year-2 destination. A 4-person team cannot run a daily + weekly + monthly + quarterly cadence while the Founder is writing, hosting, reviewing applications, and moderating the first 30 days of every Member. The Year-1 cadence is, in the long run, the daily + weekly + fortnightly cadence, and the Year-2 cadence adds monthly and quarterly as the team grows.

**Observation 7 — The 12-month Founder-capital reserve is the binding constraint.** The Financial Model's ₹15–30L Working capital gap is understated by 2–3×. The realistic 6-month burn is ₹50–65L. The 12-month reserve must be ₹1.2–1.5 Cr. A Founder with ₹60–80L pre-deployment cannot sustain the 6-month plan without a soft-quarter compromise of the philosophy. The 12-month reserve, not the 6-month burn, is the binding constraint.

**Observation 8 — The catalogue is vocabulary, not commitment.** The 48 services in the Service Catalogue are 13 v1 services + 35 future-vocabulary. The 13 v1 services are the v1 commitment; the 35 are not. A Founder who tries to deliver 48 services in 6 months is a Founder who has confused vocabulary with commitment. The catalogue must be marked explicitly.

**Observation 9 — The warm first session is the brand's most-fragile surface.** A Working Professional arriving from a screen-saturated industry, paying a premium fee, and arriving at a quiet audio room with no scaffolding will read the quiet as absence. The Floor-state visual anchor, the Host's name-use, the three-question ritual, the clear "this is what the brand is" moment are, in the long run, the brand's most-defensible single identity-formation surface.

**Observation 10 — The 4-person team can run the 6-month plan only with the right cuts.** The plan cuts the catalogue to 13 v1 services. The plan cuts the writing to 2 essays/week. The plan cuts the Founding cohort to 25–35 by Month 6. The plan cuts the second Host and second writer to Month 4–5. The plan cuts the cutover from Founder to Host at Member 25. The plan cuts the Year-2 capital raise to Year-2. The plan cuts the 4-rhythm cadence to 3 in Year 1. The cuts are, in the long run, the discipline. The cuts are, in the long run, what make the plan runnable.
