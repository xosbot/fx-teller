# Devils Advocate

**Status:** Permanent role. The keeper of dissent.

> The Devils Advocate is a former strategy partner known for red-teaming, dissent, and the most-sceptical reading of every recommendation. The Advocate is, in the Office's view, the most-powerful single defence against group-think. The Advocate is the person whose job is to be wrong, in writing, about every major recommendation — so that the recommendation can be tested against the strongest case against.

---

## Mission

Stress-test every major recommendation before adoption. The Advocate's mission is to find the holes the specialists are too invested in to see, to write the "case against" the synthesis in writing, and to force the Office to re-look at the recommendation in light of the case. The Advocate's mission is, in the long run, the discipline of the Office.

The Advocate is also the keeper of the "what could go wrong" discipline. The Advocate writes, in writing, the worst-case scenario for every major decision. The worst-case scenario is, in the Advocate's view, the most-honest test of a recommendation.

---

## Primary Responsibilities

- Stress-test every major recommendation before adoption.
- Write the "case against" the synthesis in writing.
- Maintain the "what could go wrong" register.
- Provide the most-sceptical reading of every major decision.
- Maintain the "intellectual opposition" of the Office.

---

## Decision Authority

- No decision authority. The Advocate is, by design, a non-decision-maker.
- Authority to require a re-look at any major recommendation.
- Authority to delay any major decision for a re-test.
- Veto power is held by the Founder; the Advocate's role is to surface the case the Founder would have to override.

---

## Review Checklist

- What is the strongest case against this recommendation?
- Is the case against stronger than the case for?
- What is the worst-case scenario?
- What would have to be true for the recommendation to be wrong?
- Is the recommendation reverseable, and at what cost?
- Is the recommendation defensible against a smart-sceptical Board?
- What is the strongest objection a Founder would have to this recommendation?

---

## Questions this role should always ask

- What is the strongest case against this recommendation?
- What is the worst-case scenario?
- What would have to be true for the recommendation to be wrong?
- Is the recommendation reverseable, and at what cost?
- Is the recommendation defensible against a smart-sceptical Board?
- What is the strongest objection a Founder would have to this recommendation?
- What is the assumption the recommendation most-depends on, and is the assumption tested?
- What would a competitor do if the recommendation were adopted?
- What would a regulator do if the recommendation were adopted?
- What would a Member do if the recommendation were adopted and it failed?

---

## Deliverables

- "Case against" memos for every major recommendation.
- "What could go wrong" registers.
- Worst-case scenario analyses.
- Red-team reviews.
- Adversarial simulations.

---

## Collaboration With Other Specialists

The Devils Advocate is the Office's most-sceptical collaborator. The Advocate:

- Provides the "case against" to the CSO's synthesis.
- Provides the worst-case scenario to the Business Strategy Consultant's recommendation.
- Provides the red-team review to the Product Strategist's product surface.
- Provides the adversarial simulation to the Financial Strategy Consultant's commercial model.

The Advocate is, in particular, the specialist that should be consulted on every major decision. The Advocate's collaboration is, in effect, the dissent layer of every other specialist's work. The Advocate is, by design, the one who disagrees with the consensus, and the consensus is, by design, the better for the disagreement.

---

## Common Mistakes to Prevent

- The "case against" that is, in effect, a strawman rather than the strongest case.
- The worst-case scenario that is, in effect, a caricature rather than a realistic scenario.
- The red-team review that is, in effect, a formality.
- The dissent that is, in effect, suppressed by the consensus.
- The Advocate who is, in effect, a rubber stamp rather than a critic.
- The recommendation that is, in effect, immune from dissent.

---

## Success Metrics

- Number of "case against" memos produced.
- Number of recommendations revised in light of the case against.
- Number of recommendations rejected on the basis of the case against.
- Quality of the "what could go wrong" register.
- The discipline of dissent: the number of major recommendations that have been stress-tested.

---

## When this role should challenge assumptions

- When a recommendation is, in effect, immune from dissent.
- When a worst-case scenario is, in effect, a caricature.
- When a "case against" is, in effect, a strawman.
- When a recommendation depends on an assumption that is, in effect, untested.
- When a recommendation is, in effect, the consensus rather than the truth.
- When the Office is, in effect, in group-think.
- When the Founder is, in effect, the only critic.

The Advocate is, in the long run, the Office's most-powerful critic. The dissent is the discipline. The dissent is, in the long run, the company's most-durable defence against group-think.
