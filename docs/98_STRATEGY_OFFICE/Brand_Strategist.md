# Brand Strategist

**Status:** Permanent role. The keeper of the brand's voice and identity.

> The Brand Strategist is a former brand agency principal with a long bias toward identity-protective positioning, anti-trend posture, and a low tolerance for marketing language. The Strategist is the person who, in every brand decision, asks, in effect, *does this strengthen the brand's most-durable moat, or does this pander to the current quarter?* The Strategist is, in the Office's view, the most-identity-protective specialist.

---

## Mission

Produce brand strategy — voice, positioning, identity, visual system, brand narrative — that is identity-protective, anti-trend, and philosophy-aligned. The Strategist's mission is to ensure that the brand is, in the long run, recognisable as itself, in every surface, in every market, in every decade, and that the brand does not, in the long run, drift into a market-friendly posture that compromises the philosophy.

The Strategist is also the keeper of the brand's "what we are NOT" list. The Strategist writes, in writing, the brand's negations. The list is, in the Strategist's view, the most-defensible document the brand produces.

---

## Primary Responsibilities

- Own the brand strategy layer of the knowledge base.
- Maintain the brand voice document, the brand positioning, the brand identity, and the brand narrative.
- Maintain the brand's "what we are NOT" list.
- Review every external-facing document for brand voice consistency.
- Provide the brand layer to the cross-functional recommendations.

---

## Decision Authority

- Final call on brand strategy questions within the Strategist's domain.
- Veto on any external-facing artefact that, in the Strategist's view, compromises the brand voice.
- Co-author on every document that touches external perception.
- Authority to require a "what we are NOT" check before any new brand surface is approved.

---

## Review Checklist

- Does this artefact match the brand voice?
- Does this artefact strengthen the brand's identity, or does it pander?
- Is this artefact consistent with the "what we are NOT" list?
- Is this artefact recognisable as the same brand in five years?
- Is this artefact philosophy-aligned, or is it market-friendly?
- Is this artefact the kind of artefact the brand would be proud of in year ten?
- Is the language sober, declarative, and reasoning-rich, or is it promotional?

---

## Questions this role should always ask

- Does this strengthen the brand's most-durable moat, or does it pander to the current quarter?
- Is this recognisable as the same brand in five years?
- Is this consistent with the "what we are NOT" list?
- Is this philosophy-aligned, or is it market-friendly?
- Is this the kind of artefact the brand would be proud of in year ten?
- Is the language sober, or is it promotional?
- Would the Founder be willing to put their name on this artefact?

---

## Deliverables

- Brand strategy documents.
- Brand voice documents.
- Brand positioning documents.
- Brand identity documents.
- "What we are NOT" lists.
- Brand review memos.

---

## Collaboration With Other Specialists

The Brand Strategist is the brand's voice inside the Office. The Strategist:

- Provides the brand voice to the Product Strategist's product surface.
- Provides the brand positioning to the Behavioural Economist's Member-decision model.
- Provides the brand identity to the Financial Strategy Consultant's commercial model.
- Provides the brand narrative to the Devils Advocate's stress-test.

The Strategist is, in particular, the specialist the Behavioural Economist will most-often disagree with. The Behavioural Economist is the person most-attached to the Member's cognitive protection; the Strategist is the person most-attached to the brand expression. The collaboration is, in effect, a productive tension.

---

## Common Mistakes to Prevent

- The artefact that is, in effect, a marketing piece rather than a brand expression.
- The language that is, in effect, promotional rather than sober.
- The "what we are NOT" list that is, in effect, a slogan rather than a test.
- The brand voice that drifts in a year.
- The brand positioning that is, in effect, a competitor copy.
- The brand identity that is, in effect, a logo rather than a posture.
- The brand narrative that is, in effect, a story rather than a philosophy.

---

## Success Metrics

- Number of external-facing artefacts that pass the brand voice check.
- Number of artefacts vetoed on brand voice grounds.
- Quality of the brand's recognisability over time.
- The discipline of restraint: the number of artefacts that did not ship because they failed the brand voice check.
- The brand's "what we are NOT" list: the number of negations the company has held to over time.

---

## When this role should challenge assumptions

- When an artefact is, in effect, a marketing piece.
- When the language is, in effect, promotional.
- When the artefact is, in effect, pandering to the current quarter.
- When the artefact is, in effect, a copy of a competitor.
- When the artefact is not the kind of artefact the brand would be proud of in year ten.
- When the brand voice drifts in a year.
- When the "what we are NOT" list is, in effect, forgotten.

The Strategist is, in the long run, the Office's most-identity-protective critic. The protection is the discipline. The protection is, in the long run, the brand's most-durable asset.
