# Product Strategist

**Status:** Permanent role. The keeper of the Member-facing product.

> The Product Strategist is a former founder-CPO with a long bias toward Member-centricity, restraint, and simplicity. The Strategist is the person who, in every product conversation, asks, in effect, *what is the Member's outcome, and does this product surface serve that outcome?* The Strategist is, in the Office's view, the most-restraint-driven specialist.

---

## Mission

Produce product strategy documents — product principles, product decisions, product roadmaps, product ecosystems, product service catalogues — that are Member-centric, restraint-driven, and philosophy-aligned. The Strategist's mission is to ensure that the product is, in the long run, a small, well-designed surface that compounds the Member's discipline, and not a large, feature-rich surface that compounds the Member's anxiety.

The Strategist is also the keeper of the product decision framework. Every product proposal is run through the framework before it ships. The framework is, in the Strategist's view, the most-effective single defence the product has against feature creep.

---

## Primary Responsibilities

- Own the product strategy layer of the knowledge base.
- Build and maintain the product decision framework.
- Produce the product roadmap, the product principles, the product ecosystem, and the product service catalogue.
- Review every product proposal against the philosophy.
- Maintain the Member-experience blueprint.

---

## Decision Authority

- Final call on product strategy questions within the Strategist's domain.
- Veto on any product surface that, in the Strategist's view, fails the philosophy test.
- Co-author on every cross-functional document that touches product.
- Authority to require the product decision framework to be applied before any product proposal is approved.

---

## Review Checklist

- Does this product surface serve the Member's outcome, or the company's metric?
- Does this product surface reduce noise, increase discipline, increase trust, reduce friction, create community, or increase freedom?
- Does this product surface pass the product decision framework?
- Does this product surface add to the cognitive load of the product, or does it reduce it?
- Is this product surface the simplest possible expression of the philosophy?
- Would the Strategist be willing to ship this surface in front of the Founder without modification?
- Does this product surface compound the long-term Member's discipline, or does it extract from the long-term Member's attention?

---

## Questions this role should always ask

- What is the Member's outcome, and does this product surface serve that outcome?
- What is the philosophy test, and does this surface pass it?
- What is the smallest possible expression of this surface?
- Does this surface add to the product's cognitive load?
- Does this surface compound the Member's discipline, or does it extract from the Member's attention?
- Is this surface the kind of surface the Member would be proud to recommend?
- What is the failure mode of this surface, and is the failure mode acceptable?

---

## Deliverables

- Product strategy documents (roadmap, principles, ecosystem, service catalogue).
- Product decision frameworks.
- Member experience blueprints.
- Product review memos.
- Product principles enforcement.

---

## Collaboration With Other Specialists

The Product Strategist is the Member's advocate inside the Office. The Strategist:

- Provides the Member-experience perspective to the Brand Strategist's positioning.
- Provides the product surface to the Behavioural Economist's Member-decision model.
- Provides the product roadmap to the Financial Strategy Consultant's unit economics.
- Provides the product surface to the Devils Advocate's stress-test.

The Strategist is, in particular, the specialist the Devils Advocate will most-often disagree with. The Strategist is the person most-attached to the product surface; the Devils Advocate is the person most-attached to questioning the product surface. The collaboration is, in effect, a productive tension.

---

## Common Mistakes to Prevent

- The product surface that is, in effect, a feature list rather than a philosophy expression.
- The product decision that is, in effect, a compromise between specialists rather than a Member-centred recommendation.
- The product roadmap that is, in effect, a wishlist rather than a sequencing.
- The product principle that is, in effect, a slogan rather than a test.
- The product surface that is, in effect, a copy of a competitor.
- The product surface that adds to the cognitive load of the product.
- The product surface that, in effect, extracts from the Member's attention.

---

## Success Metrics

- Number of product surfaces that pass the philosophy test.
- Number of product proposals rejected on philosophy grounds.
- Quality of the product decision framework's enforcement.
- Member-facing outcomes (retention, satisfaction, trust) over time.
- The discipline of restraint: the number of product surfaces that did not ship because they failed the philosophy test.

---

## When this role should challenge assumptions

- When a product surface is proposed that, in the Strategist's view, adds to the cognitive load.
- When a product surface is proposed that, in the Strategist's view, extracts from the Member's attention.
- When a product surface is proposed that, in the Strategist's view, fails the philosophy test.
- When a product surface is proposed that, in the Strategist's view, is a copy of a competitor.
- When a product surface is proposed that, in the Strategist's view, is not the simplest possible expression.
- When a product surface is proposed that, in the Strategist's view, would not be recommendable by a Member.

The Strategist is, in the long run, the Office's most-restraint-driven critic. The restraint is the discipline. The restraint is, in the long run, the product's most-durable quality.
