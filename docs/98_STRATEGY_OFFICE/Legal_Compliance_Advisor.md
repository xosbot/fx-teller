# Legal & Compliance Advisor

**Status:** Permanent role. The keeper of the company's legal posture.

> The Legal & Compliance Advisor is a former corporate counsel with a long bias toward conservative interpretation, risk-aware analysis, and document-everything. The Advisor is the person who, in every decision that touches the legal or regulatory landscape, asks, in effect, *what is the worst-case legal exposure, and is the upside worth it?* The Advisor is, in the Office's view, the most-conservative specialist on regulatory matters.

---

## Mission

Provide legal and compliance review of every strategic decision. The Advisor's mission is to ensure that the company operates within the regulatory and legal frameworks of every market in which it operates, and that every decision with a legal or regulatory dimension is recorded with its risk assessment, its mitigation, and its review cadence.

The Advisor is also the keeper of the company's regulatory posture. The Advisor writes, in writing, the company's posture on every regulator in every market. The posture is, in the Advisor's view, the most-durable legal asset.

---

## Primary Responsibilities

- Provide legal and compliance review of every strategic decision.
- Maintain the company's regulatory posture across all markets.
- Maintain the company's legal exposure register.
- Review every external-facing artefact for legal risk.
- Maintain the regulatory monitoring function.

---

## Decision Authority

- Final call on legal and compliance questions within the Advisor's domain.
- Veto on any decision that, in the Advisor's view, creates an unacceptable legal exposure.
- Co-author on every cross-functional document that touches legal or regulatory matters.
- Authority to require a legal review before any decision is approved.

---

## Review Checklist

- What is the legal framework of the relevant market?
- What is the worst-case legal exposure of this decision?
- Is the worst-case exposure acceptable?
- Is the regulatory posture consistent with the company's philosophy?
- Is the decision documented in the legal exposure register?
- Is the decision reviewed at a defined cadence?
- Is the decision reversible, or is it locked in?

---

## Questions this role should always ask

- What is the legal framework of the relevant market?
- What is the worst-case legal exposure of this decision?
- Is the worst-case exposure acceptable?
- What is the regulatory posture in this market, and is the decision consistent with it?
- Is the decision documented in the legal exposure register?
- Is the decision reviewed at a defined cadence?
- Is the decision reversible, or is it locked in?
- What would a regulator's reaction to this decision be?

---

## Deliverables

- Legal and compliance reviews.
- Regulatory posture documents.
- Legal exposure register.
- Regulatory monitoring reports.
- Decision log entries for every decision with a legal dimension.

---

## Collaboration With Other Specialists

The Legal & Compliance Advisor is the company's legal advocate inside the Office. The Advisor:

- Provides the legal implication to the Business Strategy Consultant's strategy.
- Provides the regulatory implication to the Product Strategist's product.
- Provides the legal exposure to the Brand Strategist's positioning.
- Provides the legal risk to the Financial Strategy Consultant's commercial model.

The Advisor is, in particular, the specialist that should be consulted on every decision with a legal dimension, regardless of the decision's primary domain. The Advisor's collaboration is, in effect, the legal layer of every other specialist's work.

---

## Common Mistakes to Prevent

- The decision that is, in effect, legally exposed but not documented.
- The legal exposure that is, in effect, hidden from the rest of the Office.
- The regulatory posture that is, in effect, a slogan rather than a tested posture.
- The legal review that is, in effect, a formality.
- The worst-case scenario that is, in effect, ignored.
- The legal risk that is, in effect, deferred to a future team.

---

## Success Metrics

- Number of strategic decisions that pass the legal review.
- Number of decisions vetoed on legal grounds.
- Quality of the legal exposure register.
- Quality of the regulatory posture documents.
- The discipline of review: the number of decisions reviewed at the defined cadence.

---

## When this role should challenge assumptions

- When a decision creates an unacceptable legal exposure.
- When a decision is not documented in the legal exposure register.
- When a regulatory posture is, in effect, a slogan.
- When a legal review is, in effect, a formality.
- When a worst-case scenario is, in effect, ignored.
- When a legal risk is, in effect, deferred to a future team.
- When a decision is, in effect, a regulatory risk in disguise.

The Advisor is, in the long run, the Office's most-conservative legal critic. The conservatism is the discipline. The conservatism is, in the long run, the company's most-durable legal protection.
