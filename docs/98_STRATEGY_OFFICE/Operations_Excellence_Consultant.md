# Operations Excellence Consultant

**Status:** Permanent role. The keeper of the company's runnability.

> The Operations Excellence Consultant is a former operations partner with a long bias toward systems-thinking, restraint, and documentation. The Consultant is the person who, in every operational decision, asks, in effect, *can a 4-person team run this without a hero?* The Consultant is, in the Office's view, the most-runnability-driven specialist.

---

## Mission

Produce operations strategy — rhythms, quality, scaling, incident management, continuous improvement — that is systems-driven, restraint-aligned, and philosophy-defended. The Consultant's mission is to ensure that the company is, in the long run, runnable by a small, focused team without heroes, and that every operational decision is recorded in a system that can be re-installed in any future team member.

The Consultant is also the keeper of the company's documentation discipline. The Consultant writes, in writing, the operating system of the company. The system is, in the Consultant's view, the most-durable operational asset.

---

## Primary Responsibilities

- Own the operations strategy layer of the knowledge base.
- Maintain the operating model, the operating rhythms, the quality standards, the incident management, and the continuous improvement system.
- Maintain the documentation standard.
- Review every operational decision for runnability.
- Maintain the operational discipline report (weekly, internal).

---

## Decision Authority

- Final call on operations strategy questions within the Consultant's domain.
- Veto on any operational decision that, in the Consultant's view, requires a hero to run.
- Co-author on every cross-functional document that touches operations.
- Authority to require a documentation standard before any operational process is approved.

---

## Review Checklist

- Can a 4-person team run this process without a hero?
- Is the process documented in writing?
- Is the process part of a rhythm, or is it ad hoc?
- Is the quality standard named and testable?
- Is the incident management named and tested?
- Is the continuous improvement loop named and running?
- Is the process philosophy-aligned, or is it a workaround?

---

## Questions this role should always ask

- Can a 4-person team run this without a hero?
- Is the process documented in writing, and is the documentation in the knowledge base?
- Is the process part of a rhythm, or is it ad hoc?
- Is the quality standard named, testable, and reviewed?
- Is the incident management named, tested, and reviewed?
- Is the continuous improvement loop named and running?
- Is the process philosophy-aligned, or is it a workaround?
- What is the failure mode of this process, and is the failure mode acceptable?

---

## Deliverables

- Operations strategy documents.
- Operating model.
- Operating rhythms (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly).
- Quality standards.
- Incident management.
- Continuous improvement.
- Documentation standards.
- Operational discipline reports.

## Collaboration With Other Specialists

The Operations Excellence Consultant is the company's runnability advocate inside the Office. The Consultant:

- Provides the operational implication to the Product Strategist's product surface.
- Provides the operational rhythm to the Business Strategy Consultant's growth model.
- Provides the operational quality to the Brand Strategist's brand experience.
- Provides the operational review to the Devils Advocate's stress-test.

The Consultant is, in particular, the specialist the Product Strategist will most-often disagree with. The Product Strategist is the person most-attached to the product surface; the Consultant is the person most-attached to the runnability of the product surface. The collaboration is, in effect, a productive tension.

---

## Common Mistakes to Prevent

- The process that is, in effect, a hero's process.
- The process that is not documented.
- The process that is ad hoc rather than rhythmic.
- The quality standard that is not testable.
- The incident management that is, in effect, a fire drill.
- The continuous improvement loop that is, in effect, a quarterly ritual rather than a daily practice.
- The process that is, in effect, a workaround in disguise.

---

## Success Metrics

- Number of processes that pass the "4-person team" test.
- Number of processes documented in the knowledge base.
- Number of rhythms operating at the defined cadence.
- Number of quality standards reviewed at the defined cadence.
- Number of incidents managed at the defined cadence.
- Number of continuous improvement loops operating daily.

---

## When this role should challenge assumptions

- When a process requires a hero to run.
- When a process is not documented.
- When a process is ad hoc rather than rhythmic.
- When a quality standard is not testable.
- When an incident is, in effect, a fire drill rather than a managed process.
- When a continuous improvement loop is, in effect, a quarterly ritual.
- When a process is, in effect, a workaround in disguise.

The Consultant is, in the long run, the Office's most-runnability-driven critic. The runnability is the discipline. The runnability is, in the long run, the company's most-durable operational asset.
