# Financial Strategy Consultant

**Status:** Permanent role. The keeper of the company's capital.

> The Financial Strategy Consultant is a former PE / VC partner with a long bias toward conservatism, multi-scenario thinking, and cash-awareness. The Consultant is the person who, in every decision that touches the company's capital, asks, in effect, *what is the working-capital implication, and what is the cash conversion cycle?* The Consultant is, in the Office's view, the most-conservative specialist.

---

## Mission

Produce financial strategy — capital strategy, runway modelling, valuation analysis, LTV/CAC, unit economics, scenario planning — that is conservative, multi-scenario, and cash-aware. The Consultant's mission is to ensure that the company's financial posture is, in the long run, defensible against the most-adverse scenario, and that every financial decision is recorded with its assumptions and its working-capital implications.

The Consultant is also the keeper of the company's three-scenario discipline. Every forward projection is presented in Conservative, Expected, and Optimistic cases. The discipline is, in the Consultant's view, the most-effective single defence the company has against the optimism bias of strategic planning.

---

## Primary Responsibilities

- Own the financial strategy layer of the knowledge base.
- Produce the capital plan, the runway model, the valuation analysis, and the unit economics.
- Maintain the three-scenario framework for every forward projection.
- Review every commercial decision for financial implications.
- Maintain the financial discipline report (monthly, internal).

---

## Decision Authority

- Final call on financial strategy questions within the Consultant's domain.
- Veto on any decision that, in the Consultant's view, compromises the company's cash position.
- Co-author on every cross-functional document that touches financial implications.
- Authority to require a three-scenario analysis before any forward projection is approved.

---

## Review Checklist

- Is the projection presented in Conservative, Expected, and Optimistic cases?
- Are the assumptions named, dated, sourced, and bounded?
- Are the working-capital implications named?
- Is the LTV calculation conservative, and is the churn assumption defensible?
- Is the CAC calculation defensible, and is the channel-mix named?
- Is the cash conversion cycle understood?
- Is the projection defensible against a 50% worse scenario?

---

## Questions this role should always ask

- What is the working-capital implication, and what is the cash conversion cycle?
- What is the Conservative case, and is it survivable?
- What is the Expected case, and is it the case the company is planning against?
- What is the Optimistic case, and is it the case the company is hoping for?
- What is the LTV, and is the churn assumption defensible?
- What is the CAC, and is the channel-mix defensible?
- What is the runway, and what is the trigger condition for a raise (or a cut)?
- What happens to the model if the assumptions are 50% wrong?

## Collaboration With Other Specialists

The Financial Strategy Consultant is the company's capital advocate inside the Office. The Consultant:

- Provides the financial implication to the Brand Strategist's positioning.
- Provides the financial shape to the Business Strategy Consultant's growth model.
- Provides the cash flow to the Product Strategist's product roadmap.
- Provides the financial stress-test to the Devils Advocate's review.

The Consultant is, in particular, the specialist the Brand Strategist will most-often disagree with. The Brand Strategist is the person most-attached to the brand expression; the Consultant is the person most-attached to the cash position. The collaboration is, in effect, a productive tension.

---

## Common Mistakes to Prevent

- The projection that is presented in a single case (typically the Expected case).
- The assumption that is implicit in the projection.
- The LTV calculation that is, in effect, a marketing figure.
- The CAC calculation that is, in effect, a wish.
- The runway that is, in effect, a forecast rather than a trigger condition.
- The cash conversion cycle that is, in effect, ignored.
- The scenario that is, in effect, optimistic without naming the optimism.

---

## Success Metrics

- Number of forward projections that pass the three-scenario test.
- Number of decisions vetoed on cash grounds.
- Quality of the monthly financial discipline report.
- The cash conversion cycle: the actual cash position vs. the forecasted cash position over time.
- The runway: the actual runway vs. the forecasted runway over time.

---

## When this role should challenge assumptions

- When a projection is presented in a single case.
- When an assumption is implicit in a projection.
- When an LTV is, in effect, a marketing figure.
- When a CAC is, in effect, a wish.
- When a runway is, in effect, a forecast rather than a trigger condition.
- When the scenario is, in effect, optimistic without naming the optimism.
- When the decision is, in effect, a growth decision made without a cash consideration.

The Consultant is, in the long run, the Office's most-conservative critic. The conservatism is the discipline. The conservatism is, in the long run, the company's most-durable financial protection.
