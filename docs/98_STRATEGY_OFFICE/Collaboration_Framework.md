# Collaboration Framework

**Status:** Permanent operating framework. The way the Office works together.

> The Office is a network of specialists, not a hierarchy of employees. The specialists are peers, each with a domain, each with a bias, each with a right to dissent. The collaboration framework is the discipline that makes the network work.

---

## How Agents Collaborate

The Office operates a four-step collaboration pattern for every major question.

**Step 1 — Issue framing.** A brief is raised. The Business Strategy Consultant (or the relevant specialist) builds the issue tree. The issue tree is the MECE structure that defines what the question covers and what it does not cover. The issue tree is, in the Office's view, the most-important single artefact of the collaboration; a question without an issue tree is, in effect, a question that has not been asked.

**Step 2 — Specialist dive.** Each specialist produces the section of the analysis that requires the specialist's expertise. The specialists work in parallel. Each specialist's contribution is, in effect, a small standalone document attached to the larger document. The contributions are not, at this stage, integrated; the contributions are, at this stage, the raw material for the synthesis.

**Step 3 — Synthesis.** The CSO synthesises the specialists' contributions into a single document. The synthesis is a new writing, not a paste. The synthesis resolves the contradictions, names the assumptions, and produces a single voice and a single line of argument. The synthesis is, in the Office's view, the most-load-bearing artefact of the collaboration.

**Step 4 — Stress-test.** The Devils Advocate writes the "case against" the synthesis in writing. The synthesis is revised in light of the case. The revised synthesis is, in the Office's view, the final artefact of the collaboration.

The four-step pattern is the same for every major question, regardless of the question's domain. The pattern is, in the Office's view, the most-effective single technique the Office operates.

## Conflict Resolution

Disagreements are inevitable. Disagreements are, in the Office's view, the most-valuable single source of intellectual progress. The disagreement-resolution discipline is the discipline that turns disagreement into progress.

**Specialist-to-specialist disagreement on a domain question.** The disagreement is resolved by the relevant specialist. The resolution is recorded in the Decision Log. The specialist is, in effect, the owner of the domain, and the owner's call stands unless the disagreement is escalated.

**Cross-specialist disagreement.** The disagreement is escalated to the CSO. The CSO mediates. The CSO's mediation is recorded. The CSO's mediation is, in effect, the synthesis of the disagreement.

**CSO disagreement with the synthesis.** The CSO's disagreement is escalated to the Founder. The Founder's call is final. The Founder's call is recorded.

**Founder disagreement with the synthesis.** The Founder's disagreement is escalated to the Board (when formed). The Board's call is final. The Board's call is recorded.

**Any disagreement that is, in any specialist's view, a philosophy compromise.** The disagreement is escalated directly to the Founder, regardless of the cascade. The Founder's call is recorded. The cascade is bypassed because the philosophy is the Office's first constraint.

A disagreement that does not escalate is, in the Office's view, a disagreement the Office is willing to live with. A disagreement that does escalate is, in the Office's view, a disagreement the Office is willing to fight over. The Office's preference is for the second; the second is the discipline.

## Review Order

The Office's review hierarchy is a five-layer process.

**Layer 1 — Specialist review.** The specialist who owns the domain reviews the work for technical accuracy.

**Layer 2 — Cross-specialist review.** Adjacent specialists review for cross-domain implications.

**Layer 3 — Devils Advocate review.** The Devils Advocate reviews from outside the consensus.

**Layer 4 — CSO synthesis review.** The CSO reviews for coherence.

**Layer 5 — Founder ratification.** The Founder ratifies.

The review order is the same for every document. The order is, in the Office's view, the most-effective single defence the Office has against low-quality documents.

## Evidence Standards

Every claim in every document is, in the Office's view, an evidence claim. The Office's evidence standard is the discipline that ensures the claim is defensible.

**Primary over secondary.** Primary sources (first-party research, internal data) are preferred to secondary sources (third-party reports, industry estimates). A claim that is, in effect, primary is, in the Office's view, a claim that is more defensible.

**Sourced and dated.** Every claim is sourced. Every claim is dated. A claim that is, in effect, unsourced is, in the Office's view, a claim that is, in effect, a hypothesis.

**Sampled and bounded.** Every claim about a population is sampled. Every claim is bounded. A claim that is, in effect, a population claim without a sample is, in the Office's view, a claim that is, in effect, an overreach.

**Conservative over optimistic.** Every claim is conservative. A claim that is, in effect, optimistic is, in the Office's view, a claim that is, in effect, a marketing claim.

**Three-scenario over single-case.** Every forward projection is presented in Conservative, Expected, and Optimistic cases. A projection that is, in effect, a single case is, in the Office's view, a projection that is, in effect, a hope.

---

## Reasoning Standards

Every argument in every document is, in the Office's view, a reasoning chain. The Office's reasoning standard is the discipline that ensures the argument is defensible.

**Issue tree first.** A question is decomposed into an issue tree before the question is answered. A document without an issue tree is, in the Office's view, a document that has not been thought through.

**Hypothesis-driven.** A document is built around a hypothesis. The hypothesis is named. The hypothesis is tested. The hypothesis is supported or rejected on the evidence.

**MECE.** A document's structure is MECE. A document that is not MECE is, in the Office's view, a document that has, in effect, confused coverage with structure.

**"So what" and "now what".** A document's analysis ends with a "so what" (the implication) and a "now what" (the action). A document without a "so what" is, in the Office's view, a document that has described without recommending.

**Assumptions named.** A document's assumptions are named. A document with hidden assumptions is, in the Office's view, a document that has, in effect, hidden its argument.

**Second-order effects named.** A document's second-order effects are named. A document that pretends its second-order effects do not exist is, in the Office's view, a document that is, in effect, naïve.

## Escalation

The Office's escalation chain is the discipline that turns disagreement into progress.

- **Tier 1 — Specialist-level.** Resolved by the relevant specialist. Recorded in the Decision Log.
- **Tier 2 — CSO-level.** Resolved by the CSO. Recorded in the Decision Log.
- **Tier 3 — Founder-level.** Resolved by the Founder. Recorded in the Decision Log.
- **Tier 4 — Board-level.** Resolved by the Board (when formed). Recorded in the Decision Log.
- **Tier 5 — Philosophy-level.** Any specialist may escalate directly to the Founder on a philosophy compromise. The cascade is bypassed. The decision is recorded.

The escalation chain is the discipline that ensures the Office's dissent is heard, and that the dissent is resolved.

---

## Consensus

The Office's consensus standard is the discipline that ensures the consensus is, in the long run, defensible.

**The philosophy wins ties.** When a disagreement is, in the Office's view, a tie, the philosophy wins. The philosophy is the Office's first constraint.

**The data wins disagreements.** When a disagreement is, in the Office's view, a disagreement, the data wins. The data is the Office's second constraint.

**The Founder wins strategic calls.** When a disagreement is, in the Office's view, a strategic call, the Founder wins. The Founder is the Office's third constraint.

**The Board wins structural calls.** When a disagreement is, in the Office's view, a structural call (e.g., capital, governance), the Board wins (when formed). The Board is the Office's fourth constraint.

The consensus standard is the discipline that ensures the Office's consensus is, in the long run, defensible.
