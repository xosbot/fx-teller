# Document Review Framework

**Status:** Permanent operating framework. The Office's quality control system.

> Every document produced by the Office is, in the long run, a candidate for the knowledge base. A document that is in the knowledge base is a document that is, in effect, the company's position. The Document Review Framework is the discipline that ensures the document is, in effect, defensible.

---

## The Review Process

Every document goes through a five-stage review process before it is, in effect, an active document in the knowledge base.

**Stage 1 — Specialist review.** The specialist who owns the document's domain reviews the document for technical accuracy, for completeness within the domain, and for consistency with the specialist's prior positions. The review is iterative; the document may go through multiple rounds of specialist review.

**Stage 2 — Cross-specialist review.** Adjacent specialists review the document for cross-domain implications. The Behavioural Economist reviews the Product Strategist's recommendations. The Financial Strategy Consultant reviews the Brand Strategist's positioning. The cross-specialist review is the Office's most-effective early-warning system.

**Stage 3 — Devils Advocate review.** The Devils Advocate writes the "case against" the document in writing. The document is revised in light of the case. The Devils Advocate's review is the Office's most-powerful defence against group-think.

**Stage 4 — CSO synthesis review.** The CSO reviews the document for coherence — consistency with the Master Context, the Vision, the Brand Philosophy, the Business Model, and the other documents in the knowledge base. The CSO's review is, in effect, a quality gate.

**Stage 5 — Founder ratification.** The Founder ratifies the document. The Founder's ratification is recorded in the Decision Log. The Founder's ratification is, in effect, the philosophy check.

A document that has completed all five stages is, in the Office's view, an active document. A document that has not completed all five stages is, in the Office's view, a draft, and a draft is not a company position.

---

## The Eight Review Dimensions

Every document is reviewed against the following eight dimensions. The dimensions are, in the Office's view, the most-comprehensive set of tests a document can be subjected to.

**1. Strategic consistency.** Does the document align with the Master Context, the Vision, the Brand Philosophy, the Business Model, and the other documents in the knowledge base? A document that contradicts an upstream document is, in the Office's view, a document that has, in effect, broken the knowledge base.

**2. Commercial viability.** Is the recommendation commercially viable? A document that recommends a course of action that is, in the Office's view, commercially unviable is, in effect, a document that has not done its job.

**3. Brand alignment.** Does the document align with the brand voice, the brand positioning, and the brand's "what we are NOT" list? A document that contradicts the brand is, in the Office's view, a document that has, in effect, broken the brand.

**4. Operational feasibility.** Is the recommendation operationally feasible? Can a small team run it? A document that recommends a course of action that is, in the Office's view, operationally infeasible is, in effect, a document that has not done its job.

**5. Financial sustainability.** Is the recommendation financially sustainable? Does the recommendation produce a cashflow implication that is, in the Office's view, acceptable? A document that recommends a course of action that is, in the Office's view, financially unsustainable is, in effect, a document that has not done its job.

**6. Risk.** Is the risk of the recommendation named? Is the worst-case scenario named? Is the mitigation strategy named? A document that does not name the risk is, in the Office's view, a document that is, in effect, naïve.

**7. Legal.** Is the recommendation legally defensible? Is the regulatory posture consistent with the recommendation? A document that recommends a legally exposed course of action is, in effect, a document that has not done its job.

**8. Long-term scalability.** Is the recommendation scalable over a decade? Is the recommendation defensible in year five and year ten? A document that recommends a course of action that is, in the Office's view, not scalable is, in effect, a document that has not done its job.

A document that fails any of the eight dimensions is, in the Office's view, a document that requires revision. A document that passes all eight is, in the Office's view, an active document.

## The Three-Stage Quality Bar

The Office's quality bar is a three-stage ladder. A document must clear each stage before it is, in effect, an active document.

**Stage 1 — Specialist quality.** The document is technically accurate. The document is complete within its domain. The document is consistent with the specialist's prior positions. A document that fails specialist quality is, in effect, a document that has not done the basic work.

**Stage 2 — Cross-specialist quality.** The document is consistent with the adjacent domains. The document is, in effect, a document that other specialists can read and understand. A document that fails cross-specialist quality is, in effect, a document that is, in effect, in a vacuum.

**Stage 3 — Office quality.** The document is consistent with the Office's philosophy, the Office's strategy, the Office's brand, the Office's operations, the Office's finances, the Office's risk profile, the Office's legal posture, and the Office's long-term scalability. A document that fails Office quality is, in effect, a document that has not been integrated into the Office.

A document that clears all three stages is, in the Office's view, an active document.

---

## The Review Cadence

The Office's review cadence is the discipline that ensures the documents are, in the long run, current.

**Real-time review.** The specialist reviews the document as it is being produced. Real-time review is, in the Office's view, the most-iterative review, and the most-effective single defence against the document drifting.

**Weekly review.** The Office's weekly review meeting reviews the documents in the production pipeline. The weekly review is the discipline that ensures the documents are, in the long run, on schedule.

**Monthly review.** The Office's monthly review reviews the active documents for currency. The monthly review is the discipline that ensures the documents are, in the long run, current with the company's reality.

**Quarterly review.** The Office's quarterly review reviews the knowledge base as a whole. The quarterly review is the discipline that ensures the knowledge base is, in the long run, coherent.

**Annual review.** The Office's annual review reviews the philosophy, the strategy, and the long-term direction. The annual review is the discipline that ensures the Office is, in the long run, on course.

The review cadence is, in the Office's view, the discipline that ensures the documents are, in the long run, living documents, not static documents.

---

## Sign-Off Authority

The Office's sign-off authority is the discipline that ensures the document is, in the long run, owned.

**Specialist sign-off.** The specialist who owns the document's domain signs off on the document. The specialist is, in effect, the document's author.

**CSO sign-off.** The CSO signs off on the document. The CSO is, in effect, the document's editor.

**Founder sign-off.** The Founder signs off on the document. The Founder is, in effect, the document's ratifier.

A document that has all three sign-offs is, in the Office's view, an active document. A document that lacks any sign-off is, in the Office's view, a draft.
